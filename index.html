CFD, Physics-Based Modelling & Machine Learning for Energy Systems

I am a mechanical engineer and MPhil graduate (Highest CWA, Department of Mechanical Engineering) from KNUST, Ghana, currently serving as Faculty Intern in CFD and Fluid Mechanics at Ashesi University and as a Research Consultant at Klerconsult. My MPhil was fully funded by the KEEP Scholarship.

My research sits at the intersection of computational fluid dynamics, physics-based modelling, and machine learning for energy systems — from building custom neural networks in Python/TensorFlow to running multi-physics simulations in ANSYS Fluent and conducting experimental multiphase flow diagnostics in Germany under a DAAD Research Grant.

My flagship work, SDCNN (Shallow Dense Convolutional Neural Network), is the first IRT-based PV fault detection study combining 5-fold CV, three baseline comparisons, Grad-CAM explainability, and zero-shot cross-domain testing — applied to Ghana's 2.5 MW Navrongo VRA Solar Plant.

02 — Research

Publications & Research Work

Peer-reviewed contributions advancing renewable energy diagnostics through applied machine learning.

Submitted 2026 · Under Review Eng. Applications of AI · 2025

SDCNN: A Shallow Dense Convolutional Neural Network for Automated Fault Detection and Diagnosis in Photovoltaic Systems via Infrared Thermography

Custom three-block CNN trained from scratch (no ImageNet) on 228 detection + 86 diagnosis IRT images from the 2.5 MW Navrongo VRA Solar Plant, Ghana. Achieves a mean detection F1 of 0.884 ± 0.064 and a perfect diagnosis F1 of 1.000 ± 0.000 under 5-fold stratified CV — benchmarked against VGG-16, MobileNetV2, and EfficientNet-B0 with paired t-tests, Wilcoxon signed-rank tests, and Cohen's d. Grad-CAM explainability + zero-shot cross-domain test on Italian dataset (137 images). Trains in under 5 minutes per fold on Apple M1, no GPU.

Achirobe, R.W., Mensah, L.D., Tay, G.F.K., Adaramola, M.S. IRT · FLIR C5CNN from ScratchGrad-CAMNavrongo, Ghana
